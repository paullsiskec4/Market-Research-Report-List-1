<p><p>The wrist orthoses market comprises two primary types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to allow some movement, providing support while promoting mobility and functionality during rehabilitation. They often feature adjustable components to accommodate changing conditions. In contrast, static orthotics immobilize the wrist, offering maximum stabilization to reduce pain and support healing in cases of injury or post-surgery. Both types serve distinct purposes, catering to varied patient needs in wrist management and recovery.</p></p>
