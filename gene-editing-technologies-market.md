<p><ul><li>Double Strand Break Repair</li><li>Engineered Nucleases</li><li>Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The gene editing technologies market encompasses various approaches, including Double Strand Break Repair (DSBR), Engineered Nucleases, and CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats). DSBR focuses on repairing DNA breaks through mechanisms like homologous recombination or non-homologous end joining. Engineered Nucleases, like Zinc Finger Nucleases and TALENs, enable precise DNA modifications. CRISPR, known for its simplicity and efficiency, allows targeted genome editing by harnessing bacterial immune systems, making it a versatile tool for research and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
