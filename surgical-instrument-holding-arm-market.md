<p><p>The Surgical Instrument Holding Arm market includes two primary types: articulated and rail-mounted arms. Articulated arms feature multiple joints that allow for flexible positioning and precise control of surgical instruments, enhancing surgeon accessibility during procedures. Rail-mounted arms, on the other hand, are fixed to a rail system, providing stability and ease of movement along a predefined path. Both types aim to improve surgical efficiency, reduce fatigue for medical professionals, and ensure optimal instrument placement throughout various surgical procedures.</p></p>
